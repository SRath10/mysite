Build Log


This whole thing was built from scratch using just HTML, CSS, and a bit of vanilla JavaScript. I wanted to make something personal instead of relying on a bloated website builder—mostly just for the fun of seeing what I could create with raw code. The goal was to keep things minimal, clean, and slightly technical, while borrowing some visual stuff(inspo) from the design language of Nothing, a company i am frankly obsessed with.


Because of that, the site sticks to a mostly monochrome palette with small accents, simple layouts, and nothing overly flashy. Everything here is intentionally simple because I wanted to actually understand the "how" behind the "what", rather than hiding behind heavy frameworks or pre-made templates.


Full disclosure: while the code is mine, I did enlist some help from LLMs for efficiency whenever I got stuck on a stubborn bug or needed a quicker way to implement something cool i thought of. That said, the design, structure, and the bulk of the work was done by me—mostly just experimenting, learning things along the way, and slowly piecing the whole thing together.


The site itself is split into a few simple sections. The Home page is a small introduction and a bit of playful terminal-style interaction. Top Shots is a curated set of some of my favourite photos, presented with a bit more space so each image stands on its own. The Gallery is a larger collection organised into categories, acting more like a visual archive of things I’ve captured over time. Camera Specs is exactly what it sounds like—a quick look at the gear I use when taking photos. And finally, this Build Log exists solely to document how the site came together(share my struggles) and to prove that yes, it was actually built the old-fashioned way: by writing the code and figuring things out as I went. [Turns out the html u learn in school isn't completly useless.]
